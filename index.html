Section 3.2 — Key Concepts

Demand

The quantity of a good that consumers are willing and able to buy at various prices. As price rises, quantity demanded typically falls (law of demand).

Supply

The quantity of a good that producers are willing and able to sell at various prices. As price rises, quantity supplied typically increases (law of supply).

Equilibrium

The point where supply equals demand. At this price, the market clears — there is no surplus or shortage.

Shifts

Changes in factors other than price (income, preferences, costs) shift the entire supply or demand curve, creating a new equilibrium.

Key Concepts

Demand shows how much consumers want to buy at each price — it slopes downward. Supply shows how much producers will sell at each price — it slopes upward. Equilibrium is where supply meets demand, setting the market price. Shifts occur when non-price factors (income, costs, preferences) change, moving the entire curve.
